Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How often should I use the AI Dermatologist? +
AI Dermatologist can recognize up to 62 different skin conditions, making it helpful for regular skin monitoring. The frequency of use depends on the risk level of your skin condition to ensure timely detection of changes.
What do the risk levels mean? +
Low-Risk Conditions
Photodiagnostics should be performed 1–2 times per month. This is sufficient for routine monitoring. A doctor’s visit is recommended for professional reassurance.

Medium-Risk Conditions
Photodiagnostics should be done 3–4 times per month. Visiting a dermatologist 1–2 times per month is advised to monitor early changes.

High-Risk Conditions
Consult a dermatologist immediately. Photodiagnostics should be performed weekly to closely track the condition.
⚠️ AI Dermatologist is an auxiliary tool and does not replace professional medical care.
Does AI Dermatologist replace a doctor? +
No. AI Dermatologist is not a medical diagnostic device and does not replace a doctor. It helps users become more aware of their skin health so they can seek medical advice early and prepared.
Who should use AI Dermatologist? +
Anyone with one or more skin spots can use the app. Users range from people with minor skin concerns to those who want to regularly monitor their skin health. Many users discover risks they were previously unaware of.
Is AI Dermatologist safe to use? +
Yes. The app is designed to be safe and informative, but it should only be used as a support tool, not as a replacement for professional medical advice.
How accurate is AI Dermatologist? +
The AI provides informative and data-driven insights, but accuracy depends on image quality, lighting, and skin type. Results should always be confirmed by a dermatologist.
Can AI Dermatologist detect skin cancer? +
The app can identify patterns that may indicate serious skin conditions, but it cannot confirm skin cancer. High-risk results require immediate medical consultation.
Does the app provide treatment or medication? +
No. AI Dermatologist does not prescribe medication or treatment. It provides general guidance and skincare routine suggestions only.
What type of images should I upload for best results? +
Use clear, well-lit images without filters or makeup. The skin area should be in focus and free from shadows or blur.
Is my data and skin information safe? +
Yes. User privacy is a priority. Images and data are handled securely and are not shared without user consent.
Can AI Dermatologist analyze skincare products? +
Yes. The app can analyze ingredients and products, explaining their purpose, safety, and suitability for different skin types.
What is a personalized skin routine? +
It is a custom skincare plan generated based on your skin type, detected concerns, and general skincare needs.
Can children or teenagers use AI Dermatologist? +
Yes, but parental guidance is recommended. Any concerning skin changes should be checked by a dermatologist.
Does the app work for all skin tones? +
The app is designed to support diverse skin tones and types, with ongoing improvements to ensure fairness and accuracy.
What should I do if my condition worsens? +
If you notice rapid changes, pain, irritation, or bleeding, consult a medical professional immediately.
Is AI Dermatologist free to use? +
Some features may be free, while advanced features may vary depending on the app version.
Can I use AI Dermatologist for regular skin check-ups? +
Yes. The app is ideal for routine skin monitoring and helps users track skin changes over time.
